# Structure Cluster Report: 2026-01-10

## Cluster Identification
Analysis of 38 high-priority candidates revealed three distinct structural clusters based on Anisotropy (elongation) and PAE Blockiness (domain modularity).

### Cluster 0: "Strain Antenna"
**Properties:**
- **High Anisotropy** (~3.60)
- **Intermediate Blockiness** (~2.28)
- **Membership:** PIEZO2, MESP2, UTS2R, PKD2, PTK7, VANGL1, TBX6, CAV1, WNT3A, GREM1, VIM, SIRT1, HOXB1, CDKN1A
- **Enrichment:** Signaling, Mechanotransduction, Segmentation.

### Hypothesis: The "Strain Antenna" Complex
The combination of high anisotropy (elongated shape) and intermediate blockiness suggests these proteins serve as long-range mechanical levers or "strain antennas" capable of transmitting forces across significant cellular distances while maintaining some modular flexibility.

**Mechanistic Role:**
We hypothesize that members of this cluster (e.g., **PTK7**, **PIEZO2**, **CAV1**) act as **Vectorial Strain Amplifiers**. Their elongated structures allow them to align with the primary strain axis (gravity/muscle tone), amplifying small deformations into significant conformational changes that trigger downstream signaling (PCP, Ion influx). In microgravity, the loss of directional strain leads to the randomization of these "antennas," disrupting the morphogenetic field.

**Testable Prediction:**
Truncation of the anisotropic domains (e.g., PTK7 extracellular repeats) will decouple membrane sensing from ciliary/cytoskeletal alignment, mimicking the microgravity phenotype (scoliosis/curvature defects) even under normal loading. Conversely, artificial crosslinking to enforce alignment should rescue the phenotype in unloading.

**Proposed Test:**
- **System:** Zebrafish *ptk7* mutants or cultured myoblasts.
- **Intervention:** Express truncation variants of PTK7 or PIEZO2 lacking the anisotropic linker regions.
- **Readout:** Assess cell alignment (PCP) and YAP nuclear translocation efficiency under cyclic stretch vs. static conditions.
