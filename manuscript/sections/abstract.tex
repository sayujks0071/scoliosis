
\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Background:} Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) affects 2--4\% of adolescents, yet no unifying mechanism explains why spinal curvature initiates at ages 11--15 or why the thoracic spine (T8--T10) is most vulnerable. \textbf{Methods:} We derive a dimensionless Bio-Gravitational Number $\mathcal{B}_g = EI/(MgL^2)$ governing anti-gravity shape maintenance and develop an Information--Elasticity Coupling framework connecting HOX-patterned developmental fields to continuum spine mechanics. Cross-species allometry (n=18 vertebrates) and AlphaFold structural analysis of 23 mechanosensor proteins validate the framework. \textbf{Results:} The normal spinal S-curve emerges as an energetic ground state requiring active metabolic maintenance. Humans occupy an ``Allometric Trap'' ($\mathcal{B}_g \approx 0.01$) where passive resistance fails. AlphaFold analysis reveals a 72\% mechanosensor--metabolic regulator anisotropy gap ($p = 0.011$), creating an Energy Deficit Window at spine length $L > 0.35$\,m (ages 11--15). Scoliosis emerges as a thermodynamic transition when this deficit forces collapse from the high-energy S-curve into lower-energy scoliotic modes. \textbf{Conclusions:} AIS is reclassified as a predictable thermodynamic scaling failure, not a random genetic error, with mechanotransduction proteins identified as metabolic intervention targets.
\end{abstract}
